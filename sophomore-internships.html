Sophomore Accounting Internships Guide

Sophomore Internship Recruiting Strategy

As a sophomore, you should now focus on securing an internship with a midsize accounting firm if possible. This is the ideal time to gain substantive experience that will strengthen your resume and prepare you for Big 4 recruiting in your junior year.

Strategic Benefits

An internship at a midsize firm during your sophomore year provides several strategic advantages:

  • Demonstrates commitment - Shows you're serious about a career in accounting
  • Provides talking points - Real client work and projects to discuss in Big 4 interviews
  • Builds technical skills - You'll learn accounting software, audit procedures, or tax preparation
  • Expands your network - Connections that can help with future opportunities
  • Sets you apart - Many students don't have internships at this stage

If You Can't Land a Midsize Firm Internship

If you're unable to secure an internship with a midsize firm, don't worry. Continue building your experience through:

  • Part-time accounting or finance-related work
  • Volunteer work with nonprofits (many need accounting help)
  • Bookkeeping or tax preparation roles
  • Any business-related internship

The key is to demonstrate continuous professional development and show that you're actively building your skills and experience.

Important: Don't Waste Time on Junior-Only Positions

Do not apply to internships that explicitly state they only recruit juniors. You will not get the position even if you have a perfect resume. Firms are strict about their class-year requirements. Instead, focus your time and energy on opportunities that are specifically designed for sophomores or open to sophomores. This will maximize your chances of success.

Key Takeaways

  • Aim for midsize firms - This is your best opportunity for substantial accounting experience
  • Apply broadly - Don't limit yourself to just one or two firms
  • Start early - Many firms recruit in the fall for summer internships
  • Maintain strong academics - A good GPA (3.2+) is important for internships
  • Leverage your network - Use professors, career services, and student organizations
  • Avoid junior-only positions - Only apply to internships that are open to sophomores

Discovery Internships

Diversity Internships at Larger Firms

Many larger accounting firms offer diversity-focused internship programs designed for underrepresented students, including programs for first-generation college students, students from diverse racial and ethnic backgrounds, LGBTQ+ students, and students with disabilities. These programs often provide:

  • Early exposure to the accounting profession
  • Mentorship opportunities with firm professionals
  • Networking events and workshops
  • Potential pathways to future internships
  • Support systems and resources for career development

These diversity programs can be excellent entry points for sophomores who meet the specific eligibility criteria. Check each firm's website for their current diversity initiatives and application requirements.

Tips for Sophomores:

  • Complete introductory accounting courses
  • Develop proficiency in Excel and accounting software
  • Build relationships with professors for recommendations
  • Start preparing for CPA exam requirements
  • Research diversity programs if you qualify - many firms offer these programs
  • Apply early - internship deadlines are often in the fall
  • Focus on midsize firms for the best opportunities
