Freshman Internships

What Matters Most: Getting Work Experience

As a freshman, the most important thing you can do is gain any type of work experience. Don't worry about finding the "perfect" internship or landing a position at a prestigious firm. At this stage, any work experience that demonstrates professionalism, responsibility, and basic business skills will strengthen your resume and help you stand out in future applications.

Whether it's part-time office work, shadowing opportunities, administrative support, or volunteer positions, any experience shows future employers that you're committed, reliable, and ready to learn. The specific company or role matters less than the fact that you're building your professional foundation.

Diversity Internships at Larger Firms

Many larger accounting firms offer diversity-focused internship programs designed for underrepresented students, including programs for first-generation college students, students from diverse racial and ethnic backgrounds, LGBTQ+ students, and students with disabilities. These programs often provide:

  • Early exposure to the accounting profession
  • Mentorship opportunities with firm professionals
  • Networking events and workshops
  • Potential pathways to future internships
  • Support systems and resources for career development

These diversity programs can be excellent entry points for freshmen who meet the specific eligibility criteria. Check each firm's website for their current diversity initiatives and application requirements.

Tips for Freshmen:

  • Focus on getting any work experience - it doesn't have to be accounting-specific
  • Apply early - many programs have early fall deadlines
  • Research diversity programs if you qualify - these can be great opportunities
  • Maintain a strong GPA (3.0+) while gaining experience
  • Join accounting student organizations for networking
  • Attend career fairs and firm information sessions
  • Don't be discouraged if you don't get an internship - any work experience helps
